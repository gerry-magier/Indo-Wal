How the day works

We go out to make the in-water experience possible. Before entering, we read the sea, visibility and whale behavior so the moment stays calm and safe.

Look

We watch for blows, backs, surface movement and the rhythm of the whales.

Read the moment

Before any possible entry, we look at swell, visibility, current, direction and whale behavior.

Stay calm

No chasing, touching or cutting across. We enter only in a way that gives the whales space and keeps the situation controlled.

This fits if

  • you are calm and confident in open water.
  • you want a calm, focused in-water experience.
  • you like small groups, a local boat and clear communication.
  • you are willing to wait for the right moment on the water.

Good to know

  • You do not need a diving certificate; this is snorkeling and calm behavior in open water.
  • The boat gets you into position before you enter the water.
  • The group stays small so entries stay calm and easy to manage.
  • The rhythm follows the whales and the situation on the water.
