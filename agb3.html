3. Subject Matter and Character of the Trip

The contract covers a multi-day Flores Expedition in Indonesia. It is an expedition trip with simple standards, high flexibility, changing local conditions and limited tourist infrastructure. The planned route leads from Labuan Bajo via Wae Rebo, Ruteng, Bajawa, the Ngada region, Kelimutu, Maumere and Larantuka to Lamalera. The return from Lamalera is deliberately flexible and may depend on weather, ferries, sea conditions and traveller preference.

5. Route and Activities

The planned route includes Labuan Bajo, Wae Rebo, central Flores Highlands toward Ruteng, traditional villages and regions such as Bena Traditional Village, Bajawa and Ngada, possible coffee plantation visits, Kelimutu area, Malange Hot Springs where possible, Kelimutu sunrise trek, Ikat weaving villages, snorkelling or boat trips around Maumere where weather and sea safety allow, travel via Larantuka to Lamalera, the sea crossing to Lamalera, village orientation and flexible return or onward travel options. The itinerary may be adapted due to weather, road conditions, ferry availability, group interests, safety and local conditions.

6. Duration, Open End and Return Transport

The minimum duration is 7 days. The stay in Lamalera and return can be flexible. Options may include extra time in Lamalera, transfer to Lewoleba and public ferry toward Maumere, independent onward travel, boat transfer back to Larantuka with bemo onward to Maumere, or return transport to Labuan Bajo where agreed and possible. Ferries and local boats can be irregular, delayed or cancelled, so travellers need enough time flexibility.

7. Group Size and Transport Comfort

The ideal group size is about 4 to 5 participants. The maximum group size is generally 6 participants per bemo plus driver and tour leader. Bemos are local, simple and may be cramped. Larger groups or more comfort may require an additional vehicle; extra costs will be communicated in advance where not already included.
