Our First Blue Whale Encounter

The magical moment that started our journey with the world's largest animals

It was a calm morning in April when we first spotted the distinctive blow of a blue whale on the horizon. That moment changed everything and sparked our passion for ethical whale watching.

After years of working as a fisherman in Timor Leste waters, I thought I had seen it all. But nothing prepared me for the sheer magnificence of seeing a blue whale up close for the first time.

The Encounter

We had departed from Dili just after sunrise, heading towards the deeper waters where local fishermen had reported unusual activity. The sea was glassy calm - perfect conditions for spotting marine life.

"In that moment, time seemed to slow down. We were in the presence of something truly ancient and magnificent."

Around 9 AM, one of our crew members shouted and pointed towards the horizon. At first, I couldn't see anything. Then I saw it: a tall, vertical spout of water vapor rising about 30 feet into the air.
